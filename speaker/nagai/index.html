Space Technology for Disaster Management

April 21, 2016 | 09:00am - 09:30am

Space technologies are essential for current disaster management. An Earth observation satellite provides images for various disaster phases such as emergency response, recovery operation, and preparedness activity. Sentinel Asia is conducting emergency observation by earth observation satellites in case of major disasters. DAN (Data Analysis Node) of Sentinel Asia analyzes the satellite data and makes value added product and discloses the result within the domestic legislation. The University of Tokyo coordinates data analysis as a P-DAN (Principal Data Analysis Node). Global navigation satellite systems (GNSS) assist all the phases of disaster management by providing precise location and navigation data, re-constructing digital elevation model, tracking the movement of people and vehicles. Several applications of space technology for disaster management are introduced focusing on Asian region.
