National Conference on Geodetic Engineering Education, Research and Development 2016 (NatConGEE 2016)

April 19, 2016
Geodetic Engineering Theater, Melchor Hall, University of the Philippines, Diliman, Quezon City 1101 Philippines

The Department of Geodetic Engineering is one of the eight departments in the College of Engineering, University of Philippines - Diliman that is committed in providing the industry and government with trained and competent engineers. As the Center of Excellence in Geodetic Engineering education, the Department is expected to lead the role in catalyzing the field of Geodetic Engineering.

As one of our missions, which is to find ways in which Geodetic Engineering Education can be strengthened, we are organizing the National Conference for Geodetic Engineering Education, Research and Development 2016 (NatConGEE 2016) on 19 April 2016 in the Geodetic Engineering Theater, Melchor Hall, Diliman, Quezon City. This one-day conference aims to create a venue for educators to discuss strategic development in the Geodetic Engineering Education, including curriculum needs, trends in R&D, technological advancements, and professional competency issues. The target participants of this conference are the faculty members of different SUCs/HEIs in the Philippines offering Geodetic Engineering course, industry-based resources speakers, and relevant government agency personnel.

I. Rationale

Geodetic engineers as the primary providers of geospatial information are considered to be among pillar professionals in resource management, infrastructure development and other activities essential to the economy. The strength and vigor of the geodetic engineering profession must therefore be firmly rooted on a strong foundation - the BS Geodetic Engineering degree program. All SUCs/HEIs with BSGE programs should be able to offer a modern and up-to-date curriculum and engage aggressively in research and development activities.

The strengthening of Geodetic Engineering as a profession needs the support of competent faculty members who adhere to a comprehensive curriculum in order to produce proficient students and graduates. In order to achieve this, it is necessary to update the GE faculty members from different colleges and universities with the current curriculum needs, trends in research and development, technological advancements, and professional matters, in order to formulate effective teaching strategies.

II. Objectives

The objectives of this workshop are:

  • Provide a venue for presentation and discussion on the developments in the field of geomatics and geospatial sciences and technology relevant to the present needs of the Geodetic Engineering profession;
  • Introduce the value of research and development in Geodetic Engineering and discuss potential areas of collaboration among universities, colleges, and key stakeholders
  • Present technological advancements in the field of Geodetic Engineering to participating faculty members that may be incorporated in BSGE courses;
  • Establish a consortium of faculty members that can convene to address present and future concerns related to GE Education, Research and Development; and
  • Promote camaraderie and linkages among faculty members from different colleges and universities, together with those in government service and private industry.

Poster Template


The guidelines for the poster presentation are as follows:

  1. The presenter is in charge of the printing of their poster.
  2. Posters should be printed on a 2 x 5 ft canvas (portrait).
  3. Fonts must be in black.
  4. Headings shall be at least 48-point font and text not less than 24-point font.
  5. Keep it simple but add enough graphs, charts, tables, pictures, etc. to make it interesting.
  6. Organize the materials properly for ease of reading. As much as possible, use light background.
  7. Use headings such as (but not restricted to) “Statement of the Problem”, “Theoretical Framework”, “Significance of study”, “Methodology”, and “Results”**.
  8. Submit the printed poster to the secretariat on the first day of the conference.
